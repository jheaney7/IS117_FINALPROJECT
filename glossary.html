Poker Glossary

Learn common poker terms to improve your game!

  • 1. Action: (1) One's turn to act during a hand. (2) To bet or raise. (3) Used to describe a game with a lot of betting and raising.
  • 2. Ante: A small bet all players are required to make before a hand is dealt.
  • 3. Backdoor: Hitting your needed cards on the turn and the river to make your hand.
  • 4. Bad Beat: When a player who initially had a substantial statistical lead over an opponent loses the hand to that opponent after the flop, turn, or river.
  • 5. Blind: The generic term for either the big blind or the small blind.
  • 6. Board: The community cards that everyone uses in combination with their pocket cards to form the best hand.
  • 7. Bubble: In a tournament, the bubble is the top finisher out of the money.
  • 8. Burn: The discarding of the top card before dealing community cards.
  • 9. Call: To match the current bet made by another player.
  • 10. Check: To pass the action to the next player without betting.
  • 11. Community Cards: Cards dealt face-up in the center of the table that all players can use.
  • 12. Draw: Playing a hand that is not yet made but could become strong if the right cards come.
  • 13. Fold: To discard your hand and forfeit interest in the current pot.
  • 14. Kicker: An unmatched card used to break ties between hands of the same rank.
  • 15. Limp: To call the minimum bet rather than raise.
  • 16. Muck: The pile of folded and burned cards in front of the dealer.
  • 17. Pot: The total amount of money or chips bet during a hand.
  • 18. Raise: To increase the current bet amount.
  • 19. Rake: The portion of the pot that the house takes as a fee for hosting the game.
  • 20. Showdown: The point at which all remaining players reveal their hands to determine the winner.
